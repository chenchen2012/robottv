# news.robot.tv Editorial Policy

This file defines the default selection standard for `news.robot.tv`.

## Goal

Publish useful robotics reporting and analysis, not generic aggregation, event marketing, or low-value promo copy.

The test is simple:

1. Did something real happen in robotics?
2. Will a serious reader learn something useful about companies, products, deployment, policy, or market direction?
3. Is the item closer to reporting than promotion?

If the answer to `3` is no, do not publish it.

## Publish Criteria

An item is a good fit when most of the following are true:

- trusted source or source material with clear editorial value
- clear robotics relevance
- real company, product, deployment, funding, policy, or technical signal
- useful to operators, founders, investors, or serious robotics followers
- strong enough to support a non-thin standalone post
- not a near-duplicate of a recent `news.robot.tv` story

## Good Candidates

These usually belong on `news.robot.tv`:

- major robotics company developments
- humanoid, quadruped, warehouse, factory, inspection, and physical-AI deployment signals
- funding rounds with real strategic significance
- important policy, defense, standards, or supply-chain developments tied to robotics
- meaningful technical advances with commercial or operational implications
- event coverage only when the event item carries real industry signal

## Do Not Publish

These should be rejected by default:

- registration pushes
- early-bird deadlines
- discount or ticket promos
- sponsor-style event marketing
- “save the date” announcements with no real robotics signal
- thin press-release rewrites with no operational insight
- duplicate stories that add no new angle
- generic AI items with only weak robotics connection

## Event Coverage Rule

Event-related coverage is allowed only when it is editorially meaningful.

Allowed examples:

- an important keynote with real robotics implications
- a major launch or public debut
- a standards or policy announcement that changes the market
- a conference takeaway that reveals deployment or commercialization direction

Rejected examples:

- registration reminders
- agenda promos
- deadline extensions
- ticket pricing updates
- venue or sponsor promotion

## Quality Standard

Every published post should try to provide:

- a clear statement of what happened
- why it matters
- what readers should watch next

If a post is too thin to do that well, improve it or do not index/publish it.

## SEO Principle

Do not publish low-value promotional pages just to create more URLs.

For Google SEO, `news.robot.tv` should prefer:

- fewer but stronger pages
- useful evergreen and timely coverage
- retired low-value slugs redirected or canonicalized toward better pages

## Automation Rule

Automation should be conservative.

Default rule:

- when in doubt between a weak/promo item and skipping it, skip it

Current operational guardrail:

- promotional event/registration posts should be blocked from auto-publish
